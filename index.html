Announcements

Stage 4 Update: Overgrown sections between Thawalanthenna - Pundaluoya have been cleared and are now fully accessible. Please continue to follow the official waymarking signs and stay on designated trail. Take extra care in areas that may still be slippery after recent rains.

All hikers and trail operators are strongly advised to:

  1. Closely monitor Met Department weather updates.
  2. Follow instructions issued by the Disaster Management Centre (DMC) and local authorities.
  3. Be prepared for possible evacuation in high-risk areas.
  4. Postpone or avoid hiking activities along the Pekoe Trail during this period.

Directional sign painting is currently underway on Stage 1. Due to weather constraints, some signs are still in progress and not yet fully completed. Please follow existing markers or The Pekoe Trail App for navigation.

Due to changing weather conditions in the Central Highlands, hikers are advised to exercise extra caution on the following stages: Stage 2, 3, 4, 9, 12, 17 & 18. Trails may be slippery with reduced visibility and sudden rainfall.

Respect the trail and its communities: Stick to marked paths, avoid littering, and don’t give money to children. Let’s support local communities in sustainable ways!
